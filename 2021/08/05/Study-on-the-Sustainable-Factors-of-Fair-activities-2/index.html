Study on the Sustainable Factors of Fair activities(2)

Sustainable Business Model

Aiming to get to know what is sustainable development of fair activities, it is necessary for us to learn what is sustainable business model.

Business model has four main characteristics： effectiveness, endogeneity, creativity and sustainability. As business environment keeps changing, sustainability seems to be more important and becomes the core objective of many companies. Achieving sustainable development requires companies not only to improve their core competitiveness, but also to have corporate social responsibility. According to Our Common Future from WCED, sustainable development needs to both satisfy the needs of contemporary people and avoid influencing the ability of developing of descendants. It is important to achieve the balance.

The Triple Bottom Line - economic line, social line and ecological line are the three mainstays of achieving sustainable development.

  1. Economic line requires enough profits that can support companies to keep running and innovating.
  2. Social line judges that whether companies benefit the society during their development or not.
  3. Ecological line judges that whether the products of companies are environment friendly or not. The pollution produced in the production process is also concerned.

The essence of sustainable business model: enterprises pursue economic sustainability (basic), social sustainability and environmental sustainability through a series of business activities.

To summarize,

First of all, satisfying business objective and achieving profit growth are the primary goals of sustainable development. (internal factors)

Secondly, companies need to keep a watchful eye on the requirements of environment, ecology, society and culture so that they can achieve harmonious development. (external factors)

Life Cycle Theory

The most special part of life cycle theory is that it considers the research object as an organism. It contraposes the difference of the “organism” in various time period and divides the whole time into birth stage, growth stage, degenerating stage and death stage.

The four stages can be changed according to different characteristics of the research object. Because the object is considered as an organism, it must share same characteristics with organism. During the development of an organism, it will exchange with outside to promote self development and change. As a result, during the research process, how the research object exchange with the outside world and the similarity of transformation are considered as a necessary.

The “organism” can be influenced by the external environment both positively and negatively. But the ultimate result is not determined by the external factors, but by the internal structure of the organization. The change in external factors can only speed up or delay the death of the “organism”.

Life cycle model:

  1. Round

It emphasizes that the life cycle of the object is end-to-end. It experiences the cyclic development.

  1. Rectangular

This kind of model is mostly used in industry research, information system research.

  1. Spiral

In this model, the boundaries of life cycle are blurred. This means the end of one stage may be at the same time of the start of another stage. This is used more in electric products.

  1. Wavy

The objects in this model are always easily influenced by external environment. So they have clear life cycle boundaries. One of the characteristics of this model is its universality. It is used more than the other three kinds of models.

Enterprise Capability Theory

Resource-Based View (RBV)

To develop the enterprise, companies need to have the abilities to create and use resources efficiently. The core idea of RBV is that the heterogeneity of resources leads to the gap of competitiveness of enterprises. To a certain extent, the resources difference is the key reason to explain the success or failure of enterprises.

Classification of resource type:

  1. Tangible resources (eg. capital resources)
  2. Intangible resources (eg. technology resources)
  3. Human resources (eg. connection and channel resources)

Strong valuable resources are the base of achieving sustainable development for enterprises.

The Core Competence of the Corporation

This is the competence of combine all the knowledge together and use them flexibly.

The features of core competence are：

Extensibility

User value

Uniqueness

Three standards:

  1. Core competence helps enterprises excavate and develop potential opportunities in the market.
  2. Core competence helps enterprises create products which satisfy consumers’ demand.
  3. Core competence is difficult to be imitated.

Enterprise Dynamic Capability Theory

This is the ability of enterprises to reconstruct the internal organization in order to adapt to external changes. It is also considered as a kind of improvisational ability which is also known as the ability of the enterprise to face challenges with purpose. To summarize, dynamic capability emphasizes that enterprises need to complete effective integration of internal and external resources through continuous learning to achieve long-term development.
